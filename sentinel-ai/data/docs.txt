NASA's Artemis Program aims to return humans to the Moon, establish a sustainable lunar presence, and prepare for Mars, using the powerful Space Launch System (SLS) rocket and Orion spacecraft, featuring international partnerships (26 nations, 43 signatories to Accords) and commercial involvement, with Artemis I (2022) testing the system, Artemis II (2026) flying crew around the Moon, and Artemis III (2027+) landing the first woman and person of color near the South Pole, emphasizing science, resource utilization (water ice), and long-term exploration via the Lunar Gateway.
